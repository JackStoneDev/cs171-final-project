Comparing Demographics

The drug epidemic has taken a much larger toll on some demographics as compared to others. White, Black, and Original American men between 35-54 years of age have significantly higher risks of overdosing than the overall population and women of that same age range.

Additionally, some states are much more adversely affected. The overdose rate in D.C. is almost double the second deadliest state, New Mexico.

Drug Usage

As is evident in the last few visualizations, certain populations are affected disproportionately by overdose deaths than others. Yet, what is immediately apparent in this graph is that drug usage rates are pretty consistent between Black Americans, White Americans, and Hispanic Americans.

We find these statistics shocking, since it appears that drug usage alone does not present a valid risk for overdosing on drugs. Rather, we believe that specific drug usage is a better measurement for overdose risk, as in cocaine and heroin will have a higher risk than marijuanna. Additionally, we believe that perhaps certain populations in certain areas are more likely to use one type of drug over another.

Therefore, while the War on Drugs is often thought of in a national context, we believe it is much more important to comabt, prevent, and treat those affected by epidemics that are prevalent on a local and regional level.

Drug Arrests by Race Population

While the last visualization displayed how drug usage has remained pretty consistent amongst Black Americans, White Americans, and Hispanic Americans since at least 2002, the War on Drugs has drastically affected Black Americans more so than any other populations with conviction rates twice the national average. These stark differences can be attributed to a number of political events since 1980, but it would be foolish to ignore that Black Americans are unjustly targeted by these policies.

We hope that the American government will soon realize the War on Drugs' failure and injustice to minority populations, and will overhaul the penal system with an effective criminal justice reform that targets all populations equally and fairly and reduces the rates in drug usage and overdoses in the years to come.
