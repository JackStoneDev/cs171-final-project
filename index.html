Comparing Demographics

The drug epidemic has taken a much larger toll on some demographics as compared to others. White, Black, and Original American men between 35-54 years of age have significantly higher risks of overdosing than the overall population and women of that same age range.

Additionally, some states are much more adversely affected. The overdose rate in D.C. is almost double the second deadliest state, New Mexico.

Using the tool to the left, we can see how some demographics are more likely to overdose than others.

Drug Seizures

Drug seizures is perhaps the most tangible aspect to the "success" on the United States' War on Drugs. Though the range of this data is limited, it shows that seizures have not been all that consistent over the last five years, and certainly does not correlate with the vast increase in ODs during this time period as demonstrated in the visualization prior. This begs us to ask: is the current War on Drugs policies all that effective in saving lives?
